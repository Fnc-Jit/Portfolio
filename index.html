Hey, I'm Jitraj Esh 👋

I'm a BCA (Cybersecurity) student, researcher, and builder based in Bengaluru, India. My work spans artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, machine learning, and large-scale software systems, with a strong focus on solving real-world problems through research and engineering.

I've built projects ranging from intrusion detection systems, LLM oversight platforms, reinforcement learning environments, and malware analysis tools to financial risk engines and social-impact applications. I enjoy turning complex ideas into practical, scalable solutions that create measurable impact.

Beyond development, I'm actively involved in research — author of a published paper presented at ICCI 2026 and currently working on a second manuscript exploring associative memory systems and precision control in AI. My interests include AI safety, machine learning research, cybersecurity, distributed systems, and intelligent agents.

When I'm not coding or researching, you'll find me participating in hackathons, contributing to open-source projects, exploring emerging technologies, and continuously learning through hands-on experimentation.

Manuscript in Preparation

Query-Conditioned Precision Control for Frozen Associative Memory (PCAM)

Independent Research · 2026

Investigates whether precision in associative-memory retrieval can be controlled through query conditioning without retraining frozen weights. The research introduces a novel precision-control mechanism, conducts multi-seed ablation studies, and demonstrates statistically validated improvements over baseline retrieval performance while proving structural limits of the benchmark.

  • Novel query-conditioned precision control framework
  • 5-seed ablation experiments with zero regressions
  • Δ +0.124 improvement over baseline retrieval
  • Statistical validation of theoretical retrieval limits
  • Manuscript currently in preparation
